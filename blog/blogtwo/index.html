Reflect on Moulthrop, S. (2003) ‘You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media (1991)’. How does it speak to the internet today? I do find it interesting how Theodor Holm Nelson's concept of "Xanadu" was far removed from what we would consider the internet today. The concept of a free structured internet is a bit strange to think about given how much of it is now only accessible when using certain Web Browsers, Google has 78.83 of the global desktop search market (Bianchi, T., 2025), hypertext as suggested has reached a kind of homogeny where most content falling under a certain category, say music, is held up by big corporations who we have little to no choice but to use if we want to access said information making the market highly restrictive. This is contrary to the vision that Nelson had that the system would set us free.
